## Building Your Capstone Wireframe
Once you're ready to put together your capstone proposal, you will be required to provide a <analogy>wireframe</analogy> representing all the views of your application. At that time, you can revisit this chapter and use the <a href="https://miro.com/app/board/uXjVMucRFUc=/?share_link_id=556612065166" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Honey Rae Repairs <analogy>wireframe</analogy></a> as an example of what to build. 
<br>
<br>
Building a <analogy>wireframe</analogy> is an important part of the planning process. It forces you to think about the flow of your user experience, identifies each <analogy>route</analogy> of your application, and gives you a visual representation of every <analogy>component</analogy> in your application. Having a well fleshed out <analogy>wireframe</analogy> will help you break down the whole of your application into smaller and more manageable tasks and ultimately speed up the development process.

### Your wireframe should:
1. Represent each <analogy>MVP</analogy> view of your application
2. Represent each MVP feature of your application
3. Be readable and easy to understand
